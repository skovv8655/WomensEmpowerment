Malala Yousafzai

Pakistani activist for female education

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai became a symbol of the struggle for girls’ rights all over the world. Nine months after she was shot for talking about girls education, she gave has become an international symbol for peace. In 2011, she won Pakistan’s National Youth Peace Prize, which has since been renamed the National Malala Peace Prize. On October 10, Malala was awarded the 2014 Nobel Peace Prize. She is the youngest Nobel winner in history.

Oprah Winfrey

Actress/Producer/Philanthropist

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Oprah Winfrey has used her position as the most influential and successful woman in media to inspire, educate and empower people of all over the world.The Oprah's Angel Network has raised more than $80 million to help educate and empower women and children to believe in themselves, to support people around the globe in pursuing their dreams… and to provide those who are underprivileged and underserved with the means and education to reach their potential. Oprah has developed programs in Africa for girls, for the violated, and for those afflicted by AIDS. In 2007, she opened the Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy for Girls in South Africa. Her goal is to educate and empower a new generation of women to help lead sub-Saharan Africa towards peace and economic prosperity.
